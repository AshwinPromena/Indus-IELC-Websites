ADMISSIONS At the Indus Early Learning Centre, Jubilee Hills, we believe in creating leaders of tomorrow by preparing children for an unforeseen future. While most students join school at the beginning of each academic year, we understand that some may not be able to do so. Based on the availability of seats, we are happy to admit students throughout the year.
ELIGIBILITY The school does not discriminate, based on race, creed, colour, gender or national origin. We do, however, limit the number of students based on our capacity to provide required learning support individually.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS 1. How different is Indus curriculum from others ? We are an IB inspired school following the early years curriculum that focuses on the developmental goals of early childhood. The program is rigorous and inclusive (meets the learning needs of every child), also caters to the abilities of students by way of differentiated instruction. 2. When was the school established ? Indus Early Learning Centre was established in the year 2011 with the Pre-Nursery, Reception, PP1, and PP2 grades. 3. What is average strength of classroom ? The average students per classroom is 18 to 20 students, handled by two teachers. A home room teacher and a co-teacher. 4. What is the teacher’s background ? Teachers are qualified postgraduates either with prior experience or trained in methods of early childhood education. Ongoing professional development takes place through the year. 5. Does this school have transport facilities ? Yes, the school does have transport facility subject to the distance and locations in the city. 6. When do the admissions open and what is the process involved ? Admission notification is published on the school website, facebook page and Instagram. Email communication is sent from the school to our parent community. Process commences in the month of October and admissions take place on a first come first serve basis. 7. How often does the school communicate with parents ? Communication takes place in the form daily transaction note, monthly curriculum newsletters, Head of Early Years newsletters, Parent-Teacher meetings and regular email communication from school as and when required. 8. Does the school have a continuity after early years and what is the process involved ? Students transition from the early years to the main school at Shankerpally is subsequent and smooth. Reapplication is not required, process is initiated and taken forward by the early years. 9. How equipped will be my child be to appear for the competitive exam in future if he is education in your schoo ? The curriculum here is a lot more intense and taken to depth. Students have greater options after graduating from the IB diploma program in Grade X and XII. 10. Do you prepare students for admission to universities in India and abroad ? Indus International School Hyderabad assists students in leveraging their good Indus experiences to seek admission to colleges and universities around the world. Applying to schools and universities is an exciting and enlightening process that allows you to examine who you are, what you value, and what you might want to accomplish with your life. At Indus, we have a dedicated team of counsellors who assist our students with placement and college counselling services.
Indus counsellors assist students and parents in researching schools, making decisions, selecting, and visiting institutions, and completing applications. Our counselling department provides comprehensive guidance and support to students throughout their application process
